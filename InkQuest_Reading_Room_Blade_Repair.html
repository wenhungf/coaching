Discussion Questions

Discuss in pairs or groups, then write your own answers. There are no single correct answers — give reasons and examples.

Wind power & the environment
  1. Offshore wind farms are often called “clean,” yet building and running them still affects the sea. What positive and negative effects might a large offshore farm have on marine life, fishing communities, and the coastal view?
  2. Some critics say that the noise, the risk to birds, and the problem of disposing of worn-out blades make wind power “less green than it looks.” How would you answer that argument?
Comparing energy sources
  1. What are the main advantages and disadvantages of wind power compared with solar, hydroelectric, and nuclear energy? If you advised Taiwan's government, which mix would you choose, and why?
  2. Wind is intermittent — it does not blow on demand. What are some ways a country can deal with this problem, and how convincing do you find them?
Resins in everyday life
  1. The same and used to repair turbine blades are popular in handicrafts — resin jewellery, keychains, coasters, and . Why do you think these materials are so useful for both heavy industry and delicate crafts?
  2. If you made resin accessories at home, which safety lessons from this article would you apply? Think about skin contact, curing, and the workspace.
Safety: heat, air & waste
  1. Curing resin releases heat (the reaction) and fumes. Why is good ventilation so important when working with resin — whether in a factory or at a hobby desk by a window?
  2. Leftover resin, sticky rollers, and off-cuts cannot simply be poured down the drain. What would responsible disposal look like for (a) a wind-farm repair team and (b) a home crafter?
People & the future
  1. “Blade doctors” often work on ropes, high above the sea. What personal qualities and skills do you think the job demands? Would it appeal to you — why or why not?
  2. Taiwan is training its own blade-repair technicians instead of relying on foreign experts. Why might a country want to “localise” this kind of skilled work?
